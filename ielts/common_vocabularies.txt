Common Words used in Listening and Reading: 

> Proponent		- a person who argues for a ciuse or proposes an idea
				- (opposite of - Oponent)
> Solely			- only
> Decade		- a periad of ten years
> Abundant		- present in large quantities
> Roughly		- approximately, about
> Conquer		- to defeat someone or something, usually by force
> Vulnerable		- easily hurt, easily influenced or attacked, susceptible
> Taxonomy		- the process of classifying living things, organizing them according to their similarities
				- a system for naming and organizing things into groups that share similar qualities
> Debris			- trash scattered around after a disaster
> Sustainability	- to last over time, the ability of something to last
> Amateur		- a person who does something for pleasure, not for money
> Foster			- to encorage the development or growth of something (relationships, skills, ideas)
> Likewise		- similarly
> Deploy		- to move (troops or equipment) into position for military action
				- (in ielts) bring into effective action
				- use, utilize, employ, exploit
> Tackle			- to make a determined effort to deal with a problem or difficult task
> Redundant		- no longer needed or useful
> Apparently		- evidently, obviously
				- apparently no, apparently obvious
> Contaminate	- to make poisonous, to pollute
				- (contamination = pollution)
> Steer away from / Steer clear of - to avoid something or someone
> Otherwise		- differently, in other way
> Assess			- to evaluate
> Compile		- to gather info from different sources and put it together
> Property		- something you own or have (lands, buildings, goods)
				- (in ielts) quality of a substance or material
> Generate		- to produce or create something (energy, ideas, income)
> Threat			- a person or thing likely to cause damage or danger
> Target			- something to shoot at, aim
> Vigorous		- strong, healthy, full of energy
> Guidelines		- general rules, principles, pieces of advice
> By-Product		- something produced in the process of making something else
> Promote		- to support or actively encourage something (a cause, product, idea)
> Outbreak		- a sudden occurance of something undesireable (a disease, conflict)
> Retention		- the ability to keep or hold something
				- (in ielts) the continued use, existence, or possession of something or someone
> Abide By		- to accept and follow a rule, decision, recommendation
> Resilience		- the ability to recover quickly from difficulties
> Decline		- to decrease in strength, quality, or importance
				- decrease, diminish
> Foreseeable	- near, predictable
				- (foreseeable future = near/predictable future)
> Outline		- to roughly describe or give a summary of the main points or structure of something
> Stem From	- to be caused by (something or someone)
				- to come from (something or someone)
> A Range Of	- a variety of, a diversity of, an array of
				- a broad selection of options
> Database		- collection of information
> Ultimately		- in the end, finally
> Enormously	- greatly or to a very large degree, huge
> Designate		- to officially choose someone to do a job	(designated as cr)
				- to officially say that a place or thing has a particular character or purpose (designated as national park)
> Incorporate	- to include or integrate a part into the whole
				- include, integrate
> Literate		- able to read and write
> Correlate		- to have a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things
> Years to Come / Generations to Come - the future years, the future generations
> Congestion	- overcrowding	(traffic congestion)
> Timber		- wood used for construction
> Collision		- crash (one moving object strikes violently against another, causes damage)
> Habitat		- the natural home or environment	(where species live and grow)
> Issue			- problem
> Cope With		- to deal effectively with something difficult (stress, challenges, changes)
> Preserve		- to keep something as it is
